<p>I like automating things.</p>

<p>Probably too much.</p>

<p>If a task repeats twice, some little manager in my brain starts looking for a script, a queue, a checklist, or a tiny software intern with a clipboard.</p>

<p>This is useful. It is also how you wake up one day and realize the intern has confidence.</p>

<p>Confidence is where the trouble starts.</p>

<p>One of my agents once drafted resume bullets that sounded clean enough to wear dress shoes. Nice verbs. Good rhythm. Very adult.</p>

<p>The problem was that some of the claims had wandered away from reality. Not wildly. Worse. Politely.</p>

<p>A broken script is annoying. A polished false sentence under my name is a different problem. That is not a formatting bug. That is a me problem with better punctuation.</p>

<p>Another time, two agents wrote to the same queue file and one task vanished. No crash. No dramatic smoke. Just gone.</p>

<p>Asking software to "please be careful" is adorable and useless.</p>

<p>Another run quietly burned about $100 per day on embeddings before I noticed. Very focused. Very expensive. The cloud does not care that I am frugal.</p>

<p>And one task once yelled <code>task.picked_up</code> for almost an hour while doing nothing useful. It had gym selfie energy. Lots of activity. No progress.</p>

<p>So my rule is simple now.</p>

<p>Automate the boring stuff. Collection. Formatting. Search. Checks. Summaries. The work that should not require a human soul.</p>

<p>Gate anything with a bill, a send button, a deploy button, or my name on it.</p>

<p>Keep the weird stuff human: taste, tone, promises, relationships, final judgment, and anything where future me might ask, "who approved this nonsense?"</p>

<p>That is not anti-automation. It is how I get to use more of it without letting it quietly become my boss.</p>

<p>Let the system carry boxes. Let it sort piles. Let it make first drafts ugly enough to fix. Let it remind me. Let it check what my tired brain missed.</p>

<p>But I still want a hand on the wheel when the thing can embarrass me, charge me, misrepresent me, or sound like a LinkedIn post that escaped a lab.</p>

<p>Maybe that is the whole lesson.</p>

<p>Do not automate everything. Automate enough that the human part has room to be human.</p>

<p>Also put spending limits on the interns. Trust me.</p>
